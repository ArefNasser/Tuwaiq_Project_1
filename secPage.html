Najran Emirate Palace

Najran Emirate Palace, a historical building located in the center of the old city of Najran in the historical district of Aba Al-Saud, on an area of about 6,252 square meters. The palace is one of the most famous heritage buildings in Najran and one of the most important buildings in the region.

Al-Okhdood

The archaeological gem of Al-Okhdood in southwestern Saudi Arabia dates back more than 2,000 years. The historic site in Najran is rich with artifacts and remnants of ancient drawings and engravings on stones, which include depictions of a human hand, a horse, a camel and snakes. The remains of a mosque can also be seen. Al-Okhdood and the story of its people are mentioned in the Buruj chapter of the Holy Qur’an, which refers to a Jewish Himyarite king burning alive thousands of his citizens for converting to Christianity.

Al-Aan Palace

Al-Aan Palace, or Saadan Palace as it once called in the past, is an ancient palace located on the top of al-Aan's mountain. It was built in 1100 AH from mud on stone foundations, in an architectural style that is characteristic of the Najran region, located in the west of Najran. It was named after the village in which it is located.

King Fahad Park

King Fahd National Park in the “Sakam” forest in Najran is one of the picturesque parks in the Kingdom, which one can have enjoyable times.The park is located within a forest that will be set up in the southern sector of the city of Najran, and has an area of about 4 million square meters, and contains green spaces and roses, along with trees of sugar and Sidr and other trees planted by the region’s secretariat.

Najran Valley Dam

The Najran Valley Dam is an arch dam on Wadi Najran about 15 km (9.3 mi) southwest of Najran. It has several purpose to include water supply, flood control and groundwater recharge. Its collects run-off and sediment in the wadi and helps release it downstream slowly throughout the year. The dam was completed in 1981, and inaugurated by the Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia Nayef bin Abdulaziz Al Saud in 1982. It is owned by the Ministry of Water and Electricity.

Hima

Hima Najran has been officially recognized by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site, during the 44th session of the World Heritage Committee in Fuzho, China. The site, the sixth to be enlisted in Saudi Arabia, is home to one of the largest rock art complexes in the world. Located in Najran, in southwestern Saudi Arabia, Hima was a conduit for caravans on the trade and Hajj routes going to and from the southern parts of Arabia, to the ancient world markets of Arabia, Mesopotamia, the Levant and Egypt.

Najran Dagger Market

The Dagger Market, one of the most famous and important popular markets in Najran, is located in the Aba Al-Saud area near the city of Al-Okhdood in Najran. The market is very popular, and it witnesses a huge volume of sales. The market is famous for selling the Janabiya, which is an Arabic dagger with inscriptions for decoration, and the people of the city use it in weddings and national occasions, as it is considered part of their traditions. It also contains a market for leather goods, silver works, mills and traditional products.

The Traditional market

The Traditional market is located near the historic Emirate Palace, there are shops selling leather products manufactured from cow, camel, sheep and goat skins after tanning and processing. Among the most prominent of those industries that people still buy to this day are the "mezeb", which is a tool for carrying the infant, and the "sabbath", which is A leather belt around the waist, and the “qatf” which is a small coffee pouch, and the “mashrab” which is a leather container for keeping water, as well as shops selling wood, textile, jewelry and silver industries.
